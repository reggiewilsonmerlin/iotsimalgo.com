What makes it different

Not a signal service.
A feedback engine.

Where most ICT-style indicators stop at "here is an FVG, here is an OB" — IOTSIM continues.

Every detected setup is placed through a custom Risk Management System, simulated bar-by-bar with your chosen entry, target, stop-loss, and partial rules — then the aggregate outcome is surfaced in a live analytics dashboard.

The result: "This exact configuration has produced a Profit Factor of X over 5,000 bars on this asset." That's not guessing. That's evidence.

Core capabilities

Everything in one indicator.

🎯

5 ICT Entry Models

Individually toggleable per direction: Displacement, Fair Value Gap, Inversion FVG, Order Block, and Breaker Block. Each one simulated with your complete rule set.

📐

22-Level Fibonacci Entry

Default 0.34 (front-runs the CE of FVGs & OBs for better fill rate). OTE 0.618 preset, Front Run options, and manual input for any non-standard retracement.

🗂️

PD Array Matrix — Targets, Partials & Stop Loss

For each of the three placement contexts, independently choose which arrays are scanned: FVG, i-FVG, OB, Breaker, Wickless, Tailless, Swing H/L, BSL/SSL, HTF IPDA H/L, HTF FVG, HTF OB. Targets can use full liquidity arrays while SLs use only structural anchors.

FVGi-FVGOrder BlockBreaker WicklessTaillessSwing H/LBSL/SSL HTF IPDAHTF FVGHTF OB
💰

3-Level Partial Profits

Four split methods: Equal Distance, Early Offset, PD Arrays (Near), PD Arrays (Grid). Two sizing methods: Equal Size and Risk Back (recover initial risk at TP1).

🛡️

6 Stop-Loss Methods

Setup Auto Calc, Adjustable R, Dollars/Ticks, PD Array Matrix, Standard Trail, and Advanced Trail (trigger-armed trailing that only arms after a specific R-multiple).

🕐

ICT Killzones & Macros

Per-zone toggles for London, NY AM, NY Lunch, NY PM, London Close, Asian. Plus 8 ICT macro windows (02:33, 04:03, 08:50... 15:15 EST). Custom Time/Day filters independent of both.

🔔

4 Alert Types — Discord, Telegram & More

Setup Detected, Entry Hit, TP1 Hit, TSL Update — each independently toggleable with a custom alert template supporting {symbol} {timeframe} {direction} {event} {price} {id} placeholders. Ready for Discord webhooks, Telegram bots, or broker execution endpoints.

🔭

HTF Scout Overlay

Overlays HTF FVG and HTF OB on the active chart. With Filter By HTF on, only setups whose origin candles intersect HTF zones will fire — top-down structural alignment that often raises Profit Factor.

The workflow

From baseline to edge — in five steps.

01

Baseline Run

All 5 models on, default settings. Run on your asset + timeframe. Read the Profit Factor.

02

Read the Monitor

PF below 1? Identify the cause: asset misalignment, timeframe, or regime mismatch.

03

Sweep Variables

One variable at a time — Entry Level, Target Method, SL Method. Record PF at each step.

04

Day & Hour Filter

Open Day/Hour Analysis. Restrict to the top sessions and hours. Often the biggest single gain.

05

Prune the Leaderboard

Disable model × direction combos with PF below 1. Keep only the reliable producers.

Insight Monitor

Six pages of live analytics — directly on your chart.

The Insight Monitor aggregates simulated outcomes in real time as price plays through each setup. No external platform needed — it lives inside TradingView.

P1

Full Stats

24 toggleable rows across Total / Long / Short columns — Win Rate, Profit Factor, Max Drawdown, Killzone Win Rate, Win/Loss Streaks, and more.

P2

Overview

Condensed headline metrics for a fast at-a-glance read without scrolling the full stats table.

P3

Day Analysis

Win rate, Profit Factor, and net PnL per day of the week. Best/worst cells colour-coded. Best hour per day shown inline.

P4

Hour Analysis

Top 3 performing hours highlighted. Worst hour flagged. Best day per hour shown — often where the largest optimisation gain lives.

P5

Recent Trades

Most recent 22 trades with Base-26 Alpha IDs cross-referenced to chart labels. Instantly audit any unusual result.

P6

Model Leaderboard

All 10 model × direction combos ranked by Profit Factor. Best day + hour per model shown. Pruning candidates identified at a glance.
